American Chop Suey

★★★★★

Entree

Prep Time: 30 mins Cook Time: 30 mins Difficulty: Medium Servings: 4 Source: Nana

Ingredients

8oz dry macaroni

½ pound ground beef

2 slices salt pork

1 small onion, chopped fine or grated

1 tablespoon vinegar

3 heaping teaspoons sugar

1 can (8oz) Hunt's tomato sauce, no salt

1 can (5.5oz) tomato juice

salt and pepper to taste

Directions

Cook macaroni to al dente, drain, and set aside.

Make small meatballs with the ground beef, plus salt and pepper to taste. Cook these in a pan (cast iron) until browned. Set aside.

Dice the salt pork finely and fry in a “regular pan” (Revere pot) over medium heat. Add onion and cook until translucent. Add tomato sauce, then vinegar and sugar, and heat through. Turn off burner and add tomato juice and salt and pepper to taste.

Combine sauce with noodles and meatballs and heat through. Serve.

If you cook ahead of time, add more tomato juice when reheating to restore consistency.

Notes

“Tastes better after sitting,” says Nana.

Reb (who learned it from Nana): Look for block of salt pork more red than white. Nana cut cheese slice thickness (1/8"–3/16"), and liked the diced pieces a little larger; Reb makes them smaller, almost minced. Used a cast iron frying and and a Revere pot. Nana grated the onion with a flat grater, large holes. For our family of four, Reb uses more macaroni (9–10 oz), more beef (1 lb.) and more tomato juice, to stretch.
