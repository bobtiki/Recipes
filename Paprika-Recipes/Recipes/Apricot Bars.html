Apricot Bars

★★★★

Dessert

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 35 mins Servings: 24 Source: Nana

Ingredients

1½ cups (180g) all purpose flour

1 teaspoon baking powder

¼ teaspoon salt

1½ cups quick oats

1 cup brown sugar

¾ cup butter

¾ cup apricot preserves or jam

Directions

Sift together flour, baking powder, and salt.

Stir in oats and brown sugar.

Cut in butter until the mixture is crumbly.

Pat ⅔ of crumb mixture into 11×7×1½-inch pan, greased with butter. Spread with apricot preserves. Cover with remaining crumb mixture. Gently pat down the top layer.

Bake in 375º oven about 35 minutes, or until browned.

Cool. Cut into bars or squares. I prefer roughly 1¾" squares (six rows of four bars comes out nicely).

Notes

Nana used to makes these when I was a kid. Adapted from a previous edition of the Better Homes & Gardens Cookbook.

Nana used a hand-held pastry cutter to cut in the butter. If you're going to use a food processor (I use 10 one-second pulses per tablespoon of cold butter), maybe do that before adding the oats, because otherwise the oats turn into oat flour. (I have not actually tried this yet — I did it with the oats included, and the mix was a little too uniform.)

If the apricot preserves are hard to spread, put them in the microwave for 20–30 seconds to make them less viscous.

If you use a non-stick pan, you can turn out the entire baked sheet onto a cutting board, and then cut it into squares from there. I cut roughly 1" squares for large groups, but I think Nana's preferred size was closer to 2" squares.

I tried making these in a smaller 8x8" pan, but they came out too thick. Surface area is key.
